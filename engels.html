history

The architecture of the Van Gogh Museum has a varied history. The main building was designed by Gerrit Rietveld and opened in 1973. The exhibition wing, designed by the architect Kisho Kurokawa, was completed in 1999. In addition to these two architects, various other designers have contributed to the rebuilding or renovation of the building. In the spring of 2014, a start was made on the construction of a new entrance on Museumplein.
